Lab 1

AI Basics: useful, powerful, and imperfect.

The first skill is not coding. It is knowing what AI can and cannot do.

What AI is good at

  • Explaining ideas in different ways.
  • Brainstorming options quickly.
  • Organizing messy notes.
  • Creating drafts, tables, plans, and practice quizzes.
  • Helping you revise and improve your own work.

What AI is bad at

  • Knowing when it is wrong.
  • Checking fresh facts unless tools/sources are used.
  • Understanding your full personal situation automatically.
  • Replacing real practice, judgment, or accountability.
  • Making ethical decisions for you.
Model

The engine

A model predicts useful text based on patterns it has learned. It does not “know” like a human.

Prompt

The instruction

A prompt is how you tell AI the role, task, context, rules, and format you want.

Output

The answer

The output is a draft. It should be checked, tested, and improved.

Quick check

Which statement is the safest way to think about AI?

Starter prompt

Explain [topic] to me like I am a 9th grader. Give me a simple explanation, one example, one analogy, one common mistake, and one practice question. Do not just give me the answer — help me think.
