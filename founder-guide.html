Parent + Teen Operating Manual

How to run the founding cohort.

This is not a school substitute and not a giant public platform yet. It is a small, polished, family-built AI learning lab designed to prove what actually keeps a teen engaged.

Abhay’s role

  • Be a co-builder, not a lecturer.
  • Keep sessions short and practical.
  • Ask “what did we build?” every day.
  • Capture friction and improve the platform.
  • Protect responsible AI use and verification.

Sahil’s role

  • Pick real interests as project fuel.
  • Use AI to learn, not to avoid thinking.
  • Challenge AI when it sounds too confident.
  • Build artifacts worth showing.
  • Give blunt feedback on what is boring or useful.

Session rule

Stop before it feels like punishment. A strong 45–55 minute build beats a forced 2-hour lecture.

AI safety rule

AI can help explain, brainstorm, organize, critique, and quiz. It should not impersonate Sahil’s thinking or submit work dishonestly.

Founder rule

Every complaint is product feedback. If Sahil says something is boring, the platform needs a better mission.

Weekly founder meeting

Every Friday, review what was built, what was actually useful, what failed, and what should change next week. This keeps the project honest.
