Reference

AI glossary in normal language.

Simple definitions Sahil can actually use while building.

AI

Software that can generate or analyze text, images, code, plans, and patterns based on learned examples.

Model

The engine behind an AI tool. It produces outputs based on patterns and instructions.

Prompt

The instruction you give AI. Good prompts include role, task, context, rules, and format.

Token

A small piece of text the model reads or writes. Long prompts and long answers use more tokens.

Hallucination

When AI produces something that sounds confident but is wrong or unsupported.

Context

The background information AI needs to give a useful answer.

Constraint

A rule or limit, like “use Spanish 1–2 level” or “do not give the final answer first.”

Output format

The shape of the answer: table, checklist, script, plan, quiz, memo, or code.

Verification

Checking whether a claim is true before trusting or sharing it.

Bias

A slant in wording, evidence, or assumptions that makes one side look better or worse unfairly.

Prototype

A first working version of an idea, built so it can be tested and improved.

Iteration

Improving something through repeated versions.
